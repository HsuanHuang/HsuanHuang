About

FoodShare is a mobile application that helps to redistribute the surplus food of individuals, families, restaurants, and supermarkets to people who might need it. By providing a platform where users can share and request for food, the food waste issue in the United States can be mitigated. From identifying the issue, we built this application based on rigorous design processes. To get the most realistic user needs, we interview the potential users in a local soup kitchen. We developed the storyboards, personas, and paper prototype before we conducted our first user testing and gained feedback to iterate the design. After that, we created the mid-fidelity prototype with Balsamiq, high-fidelity prototype with Sketch and Invision. The final user testing was conducted on people in the local soup kitchen and also some college students at Cornell.

THE ISSUE

Redistribute the surplus food will be a win-win situation for everyone

User Interview

We interviewed several people in a local soup kitchen of a church, in order to gain the real insights into their needs for food. We found that these potential users have four major needs.

1. Access to available, surplus, and free food
2. Food Donation from restaurant and supermarkets
3. Eat Healthier
4. Make friends

Design Ideation

We prioritized users’ needs and identify one primary persona, and his goals while interacting with the system. Each member worked independently to brain storm 20 designs that address the persona’s goals. Then, we chose and synthesized the best ideas into a single design idea.

Storyboarding

We created storyboards that depicts how our persona’s goals are addressed by our design.

Persona

A persona was created to give us a better target user understanding when we were generating ideas.

Paper Prototype

User testing

We had our target users and college students to test and ues our paperprototypes. We gained some critical feedbacks and iterated our design based on them. For example, the users found the history tab is confusing and the notification should be on the homepage, instead of two big buttons.

Mid-fi Prototype

After the iteration from papaer portotype, we built the mid-fidelity prototype by using Balsamiq which allowed us to slight interact with the design.

High-fi Prototype

After the iteration from Balsamiq portotype, we built the high-fidelity prototype by using Sketch. We used Invision to build the complete interaction of this application and conducted the user testing by providing the functioning prototype on mobile phones.

USER TESTING

The user testing was carried out otgauge the user’s reaction towards the application and to determine what features actually strike user’s interest. We discovered some design pitfalls that are confusing to users and iterate them accordingly. Most of the feedbacks we got were positive. The users would love to see this application been available on mobile phones and believe that foodShare can really metigate the issue of food waste in US.
