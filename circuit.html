About

This is the graduation exhibition project of my undergraduate major, Digital Content and Technology, in 2015. I was the project manager who led an interdisciplinary team of five people. Circuit was a semester-long project and went on public exhibition for a week.

Circuit is an interactive installation about memory. We interviewed 30 college students about their favorite memories and recreated the memories through videos. Users could use the device to collect the memories they found themselves echo with. Users will go through three main areas- initial, circuit, and determination - to create a mix of faces.

INITIAL AREA

In this area, participants can immerse themselves in the darkroom.
As they approaching the spotlight, their faces will gradually appear on the screen in front of them.
It stimulus the participants to focus on themselves and promote their self-awareness.

Circuit Area

This is the main area of Circuit.
Inside every door are the films we made to reflect
the collective memory of our generation.
We used LightScribe Projection on the doors.

Circuit Area

Participants can press the white button in front of them
to play the film or to switch it to the next one.

Circuit Area

There are QR codes on the door, resembling the doorplates.

Circuit Area

When the participants felt the memory echos their own life experience,
they could use their cell phone to scan the QR code to collect it.

DETERMINATION AREA

We collected the scan data in the participants' phones.
Since each memory is corresponding to its owner's face, we mixed the faces with the participant's face.
The mixture of faces is the presentation of the person's life experience and memory.

AUGMENTED REALITY

We randomly collected the memories from 32 people in our ages and recreate them in the films.
We put AR makers on these 32 people's face so the participants can scan it to see the full story of their memories.
